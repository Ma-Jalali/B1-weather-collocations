📚 More Weather Collocations to Know

Study these extra collocations — they all follow adjective + noun patterns

Collocation Type Meaning & Example
strong wind ADJ + NOUN A wind that is very powerful. A strong wind blew the chairs off the balcony.
thick fog ADJ + NOUN Fog so dense that you cannot see well. The flight was delayed because of thick fog at the airport.
bright sunshine ADJ + NOUN Strong, clear sunlight. We had bright sunshine all weekend — perfect for the beach.
scattered showers ADJ + NOUN Short periods of rain in different places, not everywhere at the same time. The forecast says scattered showers in the afternoon.
clear skies ADJ + NOUN Sky with no clouds. We had clear skies all morning, so we could see the mountains.
overcast sky ADJ + NOUN A sky completely covered with grey clouds. It was an overcast sky all day, but it didn't actually rain.
biting cold ADJ + NOUN Cold that feels very sharp and uncomfortable on your skin. There was a biting cold wind on the mountain top.
boiling hot ADJ + ADJ Extremely hot (informal). It was boiling hot in the city — over 40 degrees!
patchy fog ADJ + NOUN Fog that appears in some areas but not others. Drive carefully — there is patchy fog on the motorway this morning.
tropical storm ADJ + NOUN A very strong storm that forms over warm ocean water near the equator. A tropical storm hit the coast and destroyed many homes.
